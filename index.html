The transition from ancient to modern information systems represents a fundamental transformation in the mechanics of control. From the Nilometer's data monopoly to the Persian communication velocity, we move to the modern era: the industrialization and internalization of control. Chapter 1: The Nilometer (3000 BCE)
SENSOR: HYDROLOGICAL

The Nilometer was humanity's earliest systematic quantification of nature. By converting hydrological pressure into a fiscal signal, the priesthood performed the first transduction.
